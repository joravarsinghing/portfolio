How I Think

I started as a lifelong tinkerer, the kind of person who wanted to know why a remote, robot, aircraft, car, or machine worked the way it did.

That curiosity became mechatronics: CAD, fabrication, electronics, robotics, automation, and real-world problem solving.

Today, I combine that systems mindset with AI-assisted development. I do not just ask AI to code. I design the architecture, guide the tools, evaluate the output, debug failures, and turn ideas into working software, prototypes, and workflows.

For me, engineering is not just about components or code. It is about understanding the problem clearly, building something useful, and making technology practical.
